What Is an Exchange Rate?

An exchange rate is the value of a nation’s currency when it is traded for another currency. The relative strength or weakness of a nation’s currency has a strong impact on trade and the economy.

Understanding Exchange Rates

The exchange rate between any two currencies is commonly determined by interest rates, economic activity, gross domestic product, and the unemployment rate in each of the countries.

How Exchange Rates Fluctuate

Exchange rates can be free-floating or fixed. A free-floating exchange rate rises and falls due to changes in the foreign exchange market, while a fixed exchange rate is pegged to the value of another currency.

Examples of Exchange Rates

For example, if the exchange rate from U.S. dollars to euros is 1.07, then one euro can be exchanged for $1.07. Currency conversion impacts international trade, travel, and investments.
